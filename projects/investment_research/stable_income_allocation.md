# Stable Income Allocation Research

This file tracks lower-risk stock and bond ideas for stable portfolio construction.

## Goal

Create a steadier allocation that can preserve capital, produce income, and keep cash available for equity opportunities.

## Candidate Assets

| Category | Tickers | Role | Main Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ultra-short Treasury ETFs | SGOV, BIL | Cash parking, low volatility | Reinvestment risk if rates fall |
| Short Treasury ETFs | SHY, VGSH | Low-volatility Treasury exposure | Modest duration risk |
| Intermediate Treasury ETFs | IEF | Duration exposure, possible benefit if rates fall | Price drops if rates rise |
| Long Treasury ETFs | TLT | Rate-cut upside, portfolio hedge | High duration volatility |
| Aggregate bond ETFs | BND, AGG | Diversified bond core | Rate and credit spread risk |
| Short investment-grade corporate bonds | VCSH | Higher yield than Treasuries | Credit spread risk |
| Intermediate corporate bonds | VCIT | More yield and duration | More rate and credit risk |
| Dividend quality ETFs | SCHD, VIG, VYM | Equity income and quality stocks | Equity drawdown risk |
| Broad equity core | VOO, IVV | Long-term equity core | Market drawdown risk |

## Conservative Model

```text
50% SGOV / BIL
20% SHY / VGSH
15% VCSH / BND
10% SCHD / VIG
5% AI core equity watchlist
```

## Balanced Model

```text
35% SGOV / BIL

15% SCHD / VIG
10% VOO / IVV
5% TLT
```

## Research Notes

- SGOV / BIL are closest to cash substitutes, but yield falls when short rates fall.
- IEF is more suitable than TLT for moderate duration exposure.
- TLT can benefit if long rates fall, but it is not low risk.
- Corporate bond ETFs add credit spread risk.
- Dividend ETFs still have equity market drawdown risk.
