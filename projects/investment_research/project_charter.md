# Investment Research Project Charter

## Mission

Build a disciplined investment research system for US stocks, Taiwan stocks, US Treasuries, bond ETFs, and stable income-oriented assets.

The project should help answer:

- Which AI-era companies have real revenue and profit power?
- Which companies are only hype?
- What price ranges are reasonable watch points?
- Which stock and bond assets can support a steadier, lower-risk portfolio?
- How should research, decisions, and follow-up reviews be preserved?

## Operating Principles

1. Evidence first
   - Prefer official filings, company earnings, investor presentations, reputable market data, and transparent ETF issuer data.

2. Good company is not always good price
   - Separate business quality from valuation and entry timing.

3. Risk before return
   - Any investment memo must include downside risks, anti-thesis, position sizing, and review conditions.

4. No automatic trading
   - AI OS provides decision support. It does not place trades or guarantee returns.

5. Preserve raw and processed knowledge
   - Raw sessions, research notes, memos, decisions, and reviews should all be traceable.

## Required Outputs

Every serious investment topic should produce one of:

- Investment memo
- Watchlist update
- Portfolio policy update
- Decision log entry
- Trade review
- Research note

## Review Cadence

- Watchlist review: monthly or after major earnings
- Investment memo review: quarterly
- Portfolio allocation review: monthly
- Thesis-break review: immediately when a key assumption fails
